# Best-Selling Books Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project presents a descriptive and exploratory analysis of Amazon bestseller books from 2023 to 2025 using Microsoft Excel. The objective is to understand format dominance, pricing patterns, genre demand, author review engagement, and reader rating quality.

The final dashboard summarizes the analysis through KPI cards, pivot-based charts, slicers, and business-focused insight boxes to support decisions related to inventory planning, genre promotion, author-led campaigns, and rating-based title selection.

## Business Objectives

- Evaluate bestseller market performance across book count, format, price, genre, author engagement, and rating quality.
- Identify formats and genres with the strongest reader demand and market concentration.
- Compare pricing patterns across book formats to support inventory and promotional decisions.
- Convert dashboard findings into measurable business insights and recommendations.

## Key Insights

### 1. Paperback is the dominant book format

Paperback is the leading format with 106 books, representing 50.5% of the 210-book dataset. Hardcover is the second-largest format with 84 books, showing that physical formats remain important in bestseller categories.

### 2. Format pricing shows clear positioning

Paperback has an average price of $11.84, while Hardcover averages $13.10. Imitation Leather has the highest average price at $25.64, indicating a premium or specialty format category.

### 3. Fiction and Action & Adventure leads genre demand

Fiction & Action & Adventure is the strongest genre with 55 books, contributing 26.2% of the dataset. Reading & Writing follows with 37 books, making these two categories the strongest demand areas.

### 4. Author engagement is highly concentrated

Freida McFadden leads with 2,808,693 reviews, followed by Sarah J. Maas, Colleen Hoover, and Rebecca Yarros. The top four authors together generate approximately 8.22 million reviews, showing strong author-brand influence.

### 5. Reader sentiment is strongly positive

The average rating is 4.7. The rating band distribution shows 127 books in the High category, 68 in Medium, and 15 in Low, indicating that bestseller titles are strongly skewed toward higher-rated books.

## Business Recommendations

- Maintain Paperback as the primary inventory focus because it has the highest share among formats.
- Promote Hardcover selectively as a premium format for gifting, collector, and special-edition positioning.
- Prioritize Fiction & Action & Adventure and Reading & Writing for promotional campaigns.
- Use author-led marketing campaigns for high-review authors such as Freida McFadden, Sarah J. Maas, Colleen Hoover, and Rebecca Yarros.
- Use rating bands as a quality filter, but combine rating with review count to validate real reader engagement.
- Refresh the dashboard quarterly to keep the analysis aligned with updated market patterns.

## Limitations

- The dataset includes bestseller titles only and does not represent the full book market.
- Review count is used as an engagement proxy and should not be treated as actual sales volume.
- The analysis does not include regional, demographic, inventory-cost, or profit-margin data.
- Dashboard insights depend on the completeness and consistency of fields such as format, genre, price, rating, and review count.

## Conclusion

The analysis shows a high-quality and engagement-driven bestseller market. Paperback leads the format mix, Fiction & Action & Adventure is the strongest genre, and the average rating of 4.7 confirms strong reader satisfaction. Author engagement is concentrated among a few high-impact authors, making author-led promotion an important business strategy.

This project demonstrates Excel-based data cleaning, pivot analysis, dashboard creation, segmentation, and business insight development.
